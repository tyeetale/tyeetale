![Metaphor3d Preview](/assets/metaphor3d/preview.png)

A side project born from curiosity. My close friend from high school, Zhizhuo, was deep in research on AI-generated 3D assets. His passion was infectious — he needed help building something tangible from his research, and I wanted to explore what AI could do beyond text.

![Metaphor3d 1](/assets/metaphor3d/1.png)

This was during the tail end of my senior year at NYU Shanghai, between Coopsight winding down and Tildenn starting up. We explored different interface patterns — a Discord bot? An email system to send back and forth queries and generated assets? We racked our brains before landing on a web app. We built an MVP using text-to-3D fusion models, wrapped in a Three.js web viewer where users could describe what they wanted and see generated meshes rendered in the browser. The target was indie game developers and Roblox creators who spent disproportionate resources on custom 3D assets.

![Metaphor3d 2](/assets/metaphor3d/2.png)

The tech was fascinating but the product-market fit wasn't there. We couldn't clearly identify who the user was or what problem we were solving urgently enough for someone to pay for it. Zhizhuo continued his research, and I moved on to build Tildenn.

![Metaphor3d 3](/assets/metaphor3d/3.png)

What I took from it: AI isn't just text. There are entirely different modalities — 3D, image, spatial — each with their own constraints and possibilities. This was pre-Stable Diffusion, pre-everything. Building on capabilities that barely worked yet taught me to prototype fast in ambiguous technical territory. And sometimes the most valuable outcome of a project is knowing when to move on.
