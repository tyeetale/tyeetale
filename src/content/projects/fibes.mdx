A project with the same Coopsight team — George, Arrow, and me — exploring a different space. During COVID, with everyone stuck at home, content consumption exploded. As Gen Z watching this surge happen in real-time, we asked: what's broken about how brands and creators connect?

![Fibes 1](/assets/get-fibes/1.png)

The problem we saw: brands dumped money into influencer partnerships that felt inauthentic, while aspiring creators had no clear path to work with brands they actually used. There was no system that aligned genuine enthusiasm with brand goals.

Our concept was a loyalty-driven UGC marketplace. Instead of paying influencers cash for posts, brands would offer product rewards to creators who genuinely advocated for them. Creators received items from stores they already loved, which bonded authentic loyalty — you're not shilling for a paycheck, you're showing off something you actually use. This ranged from spicy hot ramen challenges to bespoke clothing — brands set the creative brief, creators brought the energy.

![Fibes 2](/assets/get-fibes/2.png)

We designed the full platform: brand-side challenge creation, creator-side discovery and submission, and a gamified reward system. We built an MVP and proof of concept demonstrating the flow end-to-end.

![Fibes 3](/assets/get-fibes/3.png)

It fizzled — we weren't networked in the influencer space, distribution was the hard problem, and ultimately we were still students finishing degrees. But the thinking about incentive structures — how do you align user behavior with business goals without it feeling extractive? — stuck. That question of intent mapping to action mapping to value is exactly what I'm building at Mosslayer now, just with AI agents instead of human creators.
